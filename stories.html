Timeline

Moments That Made The Shoals

1000 AD

Florence Indian Mound Built

Indigenous peoples construct the largest domiciliary mound on the Tennessee River, 43 feet high. It would serve as a ceremonial center for the Mississippi culture.

1873

W.C. Handy Born in Florence

William Christopher Handy is born in a log cabin built by his grandfather. He would grow up to codify the blues and be known as the "Father of the Blues."

1880

Helen Keller Born at Ivy Green

Helen Adams Keller is born in Tuscumbia. After losing her sight and hearing at 19 months, she would become one of history's most inspiring figures.

1939

Frank Lloyd Wright Designs Rosenbaum House

Architect Frank Lloyd Wright designs Alabama's only Wright building—called "the purest example of Usonian architecture"—for $12,000.

1959

FAME Studios Founded

Rick Hall establishes FAME (Florence Alabama Music Enterprises), creating the birthplace of the legendary "Muscle Shoals Sound."

1967

Aretha Franklin Records at FAME

Aretha Franklin arrives to record "I Never Loved a Man (The Way I Love You)" with The Swampers—launching her to superstardom and cementing Muscle Shoals' legend.

1969

Muscle Shoals Sound Studio Opens

The Swampers leave FAME to open their own studio at 3614 Jackson Highway—a former coffin factory that would become one of the most legendary recording studios in history.

1969

Rolling Stones Record "Wild Horses"

The Rolling Stones come to Muscle Shoals Sound Studio to record tracks for "Sticky Fingers," including the iconic "Wild Horses" and "Brown Sugar."

1974

"Sweet Home Alabama" Released

Lynyrd Skynyrd releases their anthem that immortalizes the Muscle Shoals Rhythm Section with the lyric "Now Muscle Shoals has got the Swampers."

1982

First W.C. Handy Music Festival

Florence launches an annual 10-day festival honoring its most famous son, the Father of the Blues. The festival continues to this day, drawing music lovers from around the world.

2002

Rosenbaum House Opens as Museum

After 60 years with the original owners, Frank Lloyd Wright's Usonian masterpiece opens to the public as a museum—the only Wright building open to visitors in the Southeast.

2017

Muscle Shoals Sound Studio Restored

With help from Dr. Dre and Jimmy Iovine of Beats Electronics, the original 3614 Jackson Highway studio is fully restored and reopens as a working studio and museum.

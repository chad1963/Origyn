Sources & Credits

Every claim is sourced. Every story is verified. Here's where we got our information and who helped us tell it right.

Our Research Methodology

ORIGYN stories are built on primary sources, archival research, and collaboration with local historians and community members. We don't rely on generic Wikipedia entries or unverified claims.

Every fact is cross-referenced with at least two independent sources. When we reference oral history or personal accounts, we note them as such. We're transparent about what we can verify and what remains legend.

Florence, Alabama

  • Florence-Lauderdale Public Library - Local History Archives
    Primary source documents including city founding records (1818), historical maps, newspaper archives (Times Daily, Florence Herald), and photograph collections. Special thanks to the archival staff for access to restricted materials.
  • Alabama Department of Archives & History
    State records, census data, and official historical documentation for Lauderdale County and Florence city records from 1818-present.
  • Tennessee Valley Historical Society
    Regional historical context, Tennessee River commerce records, and Civil War-era documentation.
  • Alabama Music Hall of Fame
    Music history documentation, FAME Studios recording logs, artist biographies, and oral history interviews with session musicians and producers.
  • Muscle Shoals Music Foundation
    FAME and Muscle Shoals Sound Studio documentation, recording session logs, and interviews with The Swampers and Rick Hall (archived).
  • University of North Alabama Archives
    Academic research on regional history, published articles on Muscle Shoals Sound, and Florence cultural heritage studies.
  • Community Contributors
    Local Florence residents who shared personal memories, family photographs, and oral histories. Individual contributors are credited on specific story pages with their permission.

General Research Tools & Standards

  • Library of Congress
    Historic American Buildings Survey (HABS), maps, photographs, and digitized newspapers for regional context.
  • U.S. Census Bureau Historical Data
    Population statistics, demographic data, and economic records used for historical context.
  • National Register of Historic Places
    Documentation for historic buildings, sites, and districts referenced in our stories.
  • Academic Publications
    Peer-reviewed historical journals, regional studies, and published research on local history and culture.

Our Commitment

No Buzzwords. Just Facts.

The apparel industry is full of vague sustainability claims. We'd rather tell you exactly what we're doing and let you decide.

Materials

Why Hemp?

We're building on hemp blends because they can reduce environmental impact compared to conventional cotton. Hemp-based fibers often have lower impacts than cotton in lifecycle comparisons - using less water, fewer pesticides, and growing more quickly.

But we're not claiming hemp is perfect. The full environmental footprint depends on farming practices, processing methods, dyeing, and shipping. We're transparent about that complexity rather than pretending it doesn't exist.

Our Current Blend

60% Organic Cotton, 40% Hemp - chosen for durability, breathability, and comfort that improves with each wash.

Manufacturing

Who Makes Our Clothes

We're working with certified supply partners and prioritizing audited standards and traceability. Our manufacturing partner Hemp Fortex presents itself as vertically integrated (fiber → fabric → garment).

Certifications & Standards

Hemp Fortex publicly lists the following certifications on its certificates pages:

  • GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard)

    Organic content and environmental/social criteria throughout supply chain

  • OCS (Organic Content Standard)

    Verification of organic material content in final product

  • RCS (Recycled Claim Standard)

    Tracking and verification of recycled materials

  • ISO 9001 Quality Management

    International standard for quality management systems

  • BSCI / Fair Trade

    Social compliance and fair labor practices

Note: Where we reference certifications, we mean facility/material certifications listed by the manufacturer—and we'll keep updating our documentation as we scale.

What We Don't Do

The Claims We Won't Make

"Eco-Friendly" or "Green"

These terms are too vague to mean anything. We describe specific practices instead.

"Carbon Neutral"

We don't buy offsets and call it neutral. We focus on reducing impact directly.

"100% Sustainable"

Nothing manufactured is truly sustainable. We're working to reduce harm, not claiming perfection.

Unverified Certifications

If we can't show you the documentation, we won't claim the certification.
